TEXT 1: We then made a list of things we’d like to have in the project, and discussed what for each of us made something a ‘good’ project. We wanted a hook, either something absurd that anchored a larger news story, or something novel in terms of format.

We were all keen on using data to tell stories that had a human impact in some way. Interestingly, we were more set on project format, identifying the specific skills that we wanted to develop over the next 12 weeks, than a specific area of research. Primarily focusing on this story offered a unique opportunity to collect data where none currently exists, with potential to unearth a story that no one else could cover due to a complete lack of data. We knew there would be collection challenges, so we immediately set up interviews and meetings to gauge participation interest should we move forward with this proposal.

That weekend, we met with climate activists from the Cardiff chapter of Extinction Rebellion, who were very keen on collecting this overlooked data. Some members did raise concerns about data protection and privacy, which we would need to address to move forward. We understood that we would likely not get participants to submit arrest data if we could not assure them it would be secure. This highlighted ethical concerns we would need to discuss with our advisors.
